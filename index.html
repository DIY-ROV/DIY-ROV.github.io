Welcome to Our DIY Underwater ROV Project!

We built a low-cost, functional underwater ROV for exploration and research. Designed for under $375, it features a wireless remote, a waterproof casing, and a camera for capturing underwater footage.

Key Features:

  • Affordable & Accessible – A budget-friendly alternative to commercial ROVs.
  • Custom Engineering – Efficient motor control, buoyancy adjustments, and durable waterproofing.
  • Easy to Operate – Wireless remote control for smooth navigation.
  • Built on Open-Source Platforms – Making adjustments easy.

Explore our design, wiring, and final product to see how we made underwater exploration possible!

Challenges

During this project, we faced several challenges including creating a waterproof casing, selecting the right motors, and ensuring proper communication between the remote control and ROV. We overcame these challenges with creative solutions and teamwork.

Cost Breakdown

The total cost of the ROV project was kept under $375. Here's a breakdown of the major expenses:

  • Motors: $100
  • Frame: $50
  • Camera: $50
  • Control System: $70
  • Waterproof Casing: $80

Project Goal

Our goal was to create an affordable, functional underwater ROV that could be used for educational and research purposes. We wanted to build a device that could operate in various underwater environments and provide valuable feedback in real-time.

Materials Used

The following materials were used in the construction of our ROV:

  • Motors
  • PVC Pipes for Frame
  • Waterproof Enclosure
  • Microcontroller
  • Wireless Communication System

Design Process

Our design process involved several iterations. We started by creating a rough prototype to test the mechanics, then refined the design to ensure proper buoyancy, control, and waterproofing. Afterward, we focused on wiring and integrating the control system.

Power System

We use a 12V battery to power our ROV. The power system is designed to provide enough current to drive the motors while maintaining a safe voltage level for the control system.

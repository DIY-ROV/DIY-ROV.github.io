Welcome to Our Project

We are a team working on a DIY underwater ROV for exploration and learning. Our project focuses on creating an affordable, remotely operated vehicle (ROV) capable of underwater navigation.

ROV Overview

The DIY underwater ROV is designed to be an affordable and functional exploration device. It incorporates waterproof motors, a lightweight frame, and a camera system for real-time feedback.

Challenges Faced

We encountered challenges in waterproofing the electronics, ensuring stable buoyancy, and achieving smooth control. Each of these problems required innovative solutions to make the ROV functional.

Project Cost

Our goal was to keep costs low while maintaining efficiency. The estimated budget was $500, with major expenses being motors, waterproofing materials, and the camera system.

Project Goal

The main objective of this project was to develop an ROV that could operate efficiently underwater and be controlled remotely, all while remaining affordable for hobbyists and students.

Materials Used

The ROV frame is built using lightweight PVC pipes. The propulsion system consists of waterproof thrusters, and the control system is based on an Arduino or Raspberry Pi.

ROV Design

The design of the ROV focuses on simplicity and efficiency. The frame is made from lightweight PVC pipes, ensuring buoyancy and durability. The propulsion system consists of three waterproof thrusters to enable smooth movement in all directions.

Power System

The ROV is powered using a 12V battery system. We had to ensure that the power source was both lightweight and long-lasting to support extended underwater operation.

Wiring and Electronics

The wiring system connects the motors, camera, and sensors to the central control board. We used waterproof connectors to protect the electronics from water damage.

Final Product

After assembling and testing all components, our ROV successfully navigated underwater, proving that our design choices worked as intended.

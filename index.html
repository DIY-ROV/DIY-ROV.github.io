Welcome to Our DIY Underwater ROV Project!

We built a low-cost, functional underwater ROV for exploration and research. Designed for under $375, it features a wireless remote, a waterproof casing, and a camera for capturing underwater footage.

Key Features:

  • Affordable & Accessible – A budget-friendly alternative to commercial ROVs.
  • Custom Engineering – Efficient motor control, buoyancy adjustments, and durable waterproofing.
  • Easy to Operate – Wireless remote control for smooth navigation.
  • Built on Open-Source Platforms – Making adjustments easy.

Explore our design, wiring, and final product to see how we made underwater exploration possible!

Challenges

Building a functional ROV at an affordable price comes with several challenges:

  • Ensuring waterproofing and durability of all components.
  • Designing a stable and controllable navigation system.
  • Balancing the budget while maintaining high-quality parts.

Cost Breakdown

The total cost of the ROV is kept under $375. Here's how the budget breaks down:

  • Motors: $100
  • Camera: $50
  • Buoyancy materials: $30
  • Controller and wiring: $80
  • Other materials (frame, sealing, etc.): $115

Project Goal

The main goal of this project is to create a functional, low-cost underwater ROV for educational purposes, allowing students to explore marine life and practice engineering and design skills.

Materials Used

  • Waterproof casing
  • Electric motors
  • Microcontroller (Arduino)
  • Camera
  • Buoyancy foam
  • Wires, connectors, and switches

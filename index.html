Welcome to Our DIY Underwater ROV Project!

We built a low-cost, functional underwater ROV for exploration and research. Designed for under $375, it features a wireless remote, a waterproof casing, and a camera for capturing underwater footage.

Key Features:

  • Affordable & Accessible – A budget-friendly alternative to commercial ROVs.
  • Custom Engineering – Efficient motor control, buoyancy adjustments, and durable waterproofing.
  • Easy to Operate – Wireless remote control for smooth navigation.
  • Built on Open-Source Platforms – Making adjustments easy.

Challenges

Building a functional ROV at an affordable price comes with several challenges. Some of the biggest hurdles we encountered include:

  • Waterproofing the components to ensure they remain functional underwater.
  • Finding affordable motors and controllers that provide the right power to the ROV.
  • Designing a reliable and stable wireless communication system.

Despite these challenges, we were able to design and build a fully operational ROV that met our performance and cost goals.

Cost Breakdown

Here's the estimated cost of building our DIY underwater ROV:

  • Motors & Propellers: $80
  • Controller & Sensors: $100
  • Waterproof Housing & Materials: $50
  • Miscellaneous (wires, glue, etc.): $45

Total: $375

Our Goal

Our goal was to create an affordable, high-performance ROV that can be used for underwater exploration, learning, and development. We wanted to make it accessible to schools, hobbyists, and anyone interested in robotics.

Materials

We used a variety of materials for our ROV build:

  • Plastic & PVC piping for the frame.
  • Waterproof motors and electronic components.
  • Specialized connectors and wiring for secure electrical connections.

Design

The design of our ROV is streamlined and compact, allowing for efficient movement and easy maneuverability underwater. We designed it with the following goals in mind:

  • Minimize drag for better movement efficiency.
  • Ensure balance and stability in the water.
  • Maximize visibility with a mounted camera and lighting system.

Power

We power the ROV using a battery pack that can run for approximately 2 hours. The power system includes:

  • Battery pack: 12V, lithium-ion
  • Power distribution board
  • Wiring and fuses to ensure safe operation

Wiring

The wiring system ensures reliable operation of all components. We used a mix of soldering and connectors to ensure secure connections:

  • Waterproof connectors to prevent shorts or failure underwater.
  • Proper insulation for each wire to prevent accidental disconnections.

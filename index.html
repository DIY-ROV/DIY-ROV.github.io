Welcome to Our DIY Underwater ROV Project!

We built a low-cost, functional underwater ROV for exploration and research. Designed for under $375, it features a wireless remote, a waterproof casing, and a camera for capturing underwater footage.

Key Features:

  • Affordable & Accessible – A budget-friendly alternative to commercial ROVs.
  • Custom Engineering – Efficient motor control, buoyancy adjustments, and durable waterproofing.
  • Easy to Operate – Wireless remote control for smooth navigation.
  • Built on Open-Source Platforms – Making adjustments easy.

Explore our design, wiring, and final product to see how we made underwater exploration possible!

Challenges We Faced

Building the ROV came with several challenges including waterproofing, achieving stable buoyancy, and managing electrical components. The wiring and control systems were tricky, but our team's persistence paid off!

Cost Breakdown

The total cost of building our ROV was under $375. This includes materials, tools, and electronics required to bring the project to life. Here's a quick breakdown:

  • Motors & Thrusters: $100
  • Wiring & Connectors: $50
  • Frame & Buoyancy Materials: $75
  • Control & Power System: $100
  • Camera & Accessories: $50

Our Goal

The goal of this project was to build an affordable, reliable, and functional underwater ROV. The objective was to explore cost-effective solutions for underwater exploration and create a functional prototype with minimal resources.

Materials Used

Here’s a list of the materials used in the construction of our ROV:

  • Waterproof Case
  • Motors
  • Thrusters
  • Raspberry Pi
  • Camera

Design

We focused on a simple, modular design for easy assembly and troubleshooting. The frame was constructed with lightweight PVC pipes, and the thrusters were placed to allow easy maneuverability in all directions.

Power System

The ROV is powered using a combination of rechargeable batteries and a power distribution board. We opted for a simple setup that ensures reliable performance while keeping the design minimal and efficient.
